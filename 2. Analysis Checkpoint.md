# Analysis Checkpoint

This project followed a structured root cause analysis approach to identify the primary drivers behind declining sales.

## Checkpoint 1: Is Revenue Actually Declining?

### Observation

- Sales showed a consistent month-over-month decline.
- The latest month recorded approximately 42% lower sales than October 2017.

### Conclusion

The business is experiencing a genuine revenue decline and requires further investigation into the underlying causes.

---

## Checkpoint 2: Are Customers Spending Less?

### Metrics Analyzed

- Average User Value (AUV)
- Median Rating

### Observation

- AUV remained stable at approximately 12K.
- Median customer rating remained above 4.

### Conclusion

Customers are not reducing their spending and satisfaction levels remain stable.

**Hypothesis Rejected:** Revenue decline is not caused by lower spending per customer.

---

## Checkpoint 3: Is User Churn Driving Sales Decline?

### Metrics Analyzed

- Monthly Users
- Monthly Sales

### Observation

- User decline closely mirrored sales decline.
- AUV remained largely unchanged.

### Conclusion

Revenue decline is primarily driven by a reduction in active users rather than reduced customer spending.

---

## Checkpoint 4: Is the Decline Consistent Across All Cities?

### Metrics Analyzed

- City-wise Sales Trends
- City-wise User Trends

### Observation

- Bangalore showed approximately 97% growth compared to October 2017.
- Several cities experienced significant decline.

### Conclusion

The decline is concentrated within specific markets rather than being a platform-wide issue.

---

## Checkpoint 5: Are Certain User Segments Churning More Than Others?

### Metrics Analyzed

- User Cohort Analysis
- Demographic Distribution

### Observation

- User decline was observed across multiple demographic segments.
- City-level distribution changed significantly over time.

### Conclusion

The problem is not isolated to a specific customer segment. Geographic concentration appears to be a stronger driver of churn.

---

## Checkpoint 6: What Is Driving User Churn?

### Metrics Analyzed

- Outlet Trends
- User Trends

### Observation

- Outlet decline closely matched user decline patterns.
- Cities with higher outlet closures also experienced larger user losses.

### Conclusion

Users are leaving because restaurants are leaving the platform.

**Root Cause Identified:** Outlet churn is the primary driver of user churn and revenue decline.

---

## Checkpoint 7: Which Markets Require Immediate Attention?

### Metrics Analyzed

- Outlet Performance by City
- User Performance by City

### Observation

- Delhi experienced approximately 36% outlet decline.
- Significant user loss followed outlet closures.

### Conclusion

Delhi represents the highest-priority market for recovery and restaurant acquisition efforts.

---

## Checkpoint 8: Which Product Categories Are Most Impacted?

### Metrics Analyzed

- Cuisine-Level Performance

### Observation

The largest declines were observed in:

- North Indian Cuisine
- Chinese Cuisine
- Indian Cuisine

Each category declined by approximately 50%.

### Conclusion

Supply-side recovery should prioritize onboarding and retaining restaurants within these cuisine categories.

---

## Final Business Conclusion

The analysis confirms that the decline in sales is not caused by lower customer spending or poor customer satisfaction.

### Root Cause Chain

Outlet Closures → User Churn → Sales Decline

### Recommended Business Actions

- Recover previously closed restaurant partners.
- Introduce retention-focused commercial programs.
- Expand supply in high-demand cuisine categories.
- Prioritize restaurant acquisition in declining cities such as Delhi.
- Build outlet retention and churn monitoring frameworks.

By addressing outlet churn, the platform can improve user retention and restore sustainable revenue growth.
