## Executive Summary

- Analyzed Zomato marketplace data (Oct 2017–Jun 2020) to identify the root cause of declining sales by evaluating customer behavior, outlet performance, cuisine trends, and city-level marketplace dynamics.
- Cleaned and transformed transactional data, designed business KPIs, and built interactive Tableau dashboards to monitor **Sales Amount, Sales Quantity, Average User Value (AUV), Users, Outlets, Ratings, Customer Demographics, City Contribution,** and **Cuisine Performance**.
- Performed **trend analysis, cohort analysis, customer segmentation, geo-spatial analysis,** and **root cause analysis** to uncover factors driving marketplace decline and user churn. 
- Identified a **42% decline in sales** compared to October 2017, while **Average User Value (~12K)** and **median customer ratings (>4)** remained stable, indicating that declining revenue was not driven by reduced customer spending. 
- Discovered that **customer churn** closely mirrored the decline in sales, establishing user loss as the primary revenue driver rather than lower order values or customer satisfaction.
- Found a strong relationship between **outlet closures** and customer churn, concluding that the marketplace faced a **supply-side problem**, where restaurant attrition led to declining users and revenue.
- Revealed significant regional variation, with **Bangalore recording approximately 97% sales growth**, while **Delhi experienced nearly 36% outlet decline**, highlighting city-level differences in marketplace performance.
- Identified **North Indian, Chinese, and Indian cuisines** as the most impacted categories, each declining by approximately **50%**, providing direction for restaurant acquisition and category-specific growth strategies.
- Delivered actionable recommendations to improve **restaurant retention, outlet acquisition, customer retention, marketplace expansion,** and **long-term revenue growth**, demonstrating how business intelligence can drive strategic decision-making in a two-sided marketplace.
