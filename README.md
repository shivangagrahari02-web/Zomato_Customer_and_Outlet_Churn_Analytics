## Executive Summary

Zomato operates in a fast-moving delivery and restaurant marketplace where customer churn and outlet churn can directly affect revenue, repeat orders, and platform stability. To understand the patterns behind this churn, I analyzed transaction, customer, and outlet-level data to identify what drives attrition and where retention can be improved.

Study customer and outlet behavior, measure churn patterns, and find the operational and business factors that influence repeat usage, outlet retention, and overall platform performance.
 
Used PostgreSQL and SQL-based analysis to clean and structure the data, then applied joins, aggregations, segmentation logic, ranking, and trend analysis to compare customer behavior, outlet activity, order patterns, and revenue contribution. The analysis was built to move from raw data to business insight, not just query output.

The analysis highlighted churn-related behavior across customers and outlets, helped identify segments with weaker repeat activity, and showed where retention efforts could be focused more effectively. The project strengthened my ability to use SQL for marketplace analytics, churn diagnosis, and business decision-making in a platform setting.
